About Me

During my time in high school, I was drawn to my math classes the most, especially my AP Statistics class. The conclusions that can be reached from data, evidence, and knowledge of probability had always been of great interest to me, and once I explored Statistics in college, I was hooked. I went out of my way to ensure that I could declare Statistics as my major as soon as possible, and learned both Calculus I and II over the summer, as well as an additional Statistics course, to ensure it could happen.

However, it was not only Statistics that had caught my eye in college. Thanks to the suggestions of my friends and peers, I considered trying coding, and I am so happy that I did. Even with just putting together this website, I am having such a good time. Coding is not only technical, it is also creative and powerful! Just like statistics, Computer Science has drawn me in, and it's pushed me to teach myself HTML, Python, and various Python libraries, so that I may learn even more.

Even More About Me

Throughout high school, I participated in Mathletes, Marching Band, Model UN, and volunteered with the Boy Scouts of America for 3 summers. I've always loved trying new and different things, especially when they open a path to things I hadn't considered before.

After graduating high school in 2021, I came to the University of Illinois in Urbana-Champaign searching for what to dedicate my time to. Little did I know that my time in college would be (and is) one of the most enriching periods of my life! Not only am I playing flute with the university's Latin American Music ensemble, but I am still attending conferences with the Model UN team, and am Vice President of the university's Quizbowl Team.
